Today we're going to look at an important area of science, namely nanotechnology. 今天我们要探讨科学领域中的一个重要方向，也就是纳米技术（nanotechnology）。
So what is it? Nano means tiny, so it's science and engineering on the scale of atoms and molecules. 那么什么是纳米技术呢？“nano”意为“极小的”，因此它指的是在原子和分子尺度上进行的科学研究和工程技术。
The idea is that by controlling and rearranging atoms, you can literally create anything. 其核心理念是：通过控制并重新排列原子，你几乎可以创造任何东西。（literally 在这里表示“真正意义上地、理论上地”）
However, as we'll see, the science of the small has some big implications affecting us in many ways. 不过正如我们将会看到的，研究微观世界的科学却会带来巨大的影响，并在许多方面改变我们的生活。（implication = 影响、后果）
There's no doubt that nanotechnology promises so much for civilisation. 毫无疑问，纳米技术为人类文明带来了巨大的发展前景。（promise 作动词表示“预示着、带来希望”）
However, all new technologies have their teething problems. 然而，所有新技术在发展初期都会遇到一些问题。（teething problems = 初期困难、成长中的问题，IELTS常考）
And with nanotechnology, society often gets the wrong idea about its capabilities. 而对于纳米技术，社会大众往往会误解它的能力和作用。（capability = 能力、潜力）
Numerous science-fiction books and movies have raised people's fears about nanotechnology - with scenarios such as inserting little nano-robots into your body that monitor everything you do without you realising it, or self-replicating nano-robots that eventually take over the world. 大量科幻小说和电影激发了人们对纳米技术的担忧，例如把微型纳米机器人植入人体，在你毫不知情的情况下监控你的一举一动；或者能够自我复制的纳米机器人最终统治世界。（self-replicating = 自我复制的）
So how do we safeguard such a potentially powerful technology? 那么，我们该如何确保这样一种潜力巨大的技术被安全使用呢？（safeguard = 保护、防范风险）
Some scientists recommend that nano-particles be treated as new chemicals with separate safety tests and clear labelling. 一些科学家建议把纳米颗粒视为一种全新的化学物质，对其进行独立的安全测试，并明确标注相关信息。（labelling = 标签说明）
They believe that greater care should also be taken with nano-particles in laboratories and factories. 他们认为，在实验室和工厂中处理纳米颗粒时，也应该采取更严格的安全措施。
Others have called for a withdrawal of new nano products such as cosmetics and a temporary halt to many kinds of nanotech research. 另一些人则呼吁暂停推出新的纳米产品（例如化妆品），并暂时中止许多纳米技术研究项目。（call for = 呼吁；halt = 暂停）
But as far as I'm concerned there's a need to plough ahead with the discoveries and applications of nanotechnology. 但在我看来，我们有必要继续推进纳米技术的发现和应用。（plough ahead = 坚持推进、继续前进，IELTS高频短语）
I really believe that most scientists would welcome a way to guard against unethical uses of such technology. 我相信，大多数科学家都乐于看到能够防止这项技术被不道德利用的方法出现。（guard against = 防范）
We can't go around thinking that all innovation is bad, all advancement is bad. 我们不能一味认为所有创新都是坏事，所有进步都是坏事。（go around doing = 总是这样做/这样想）
As with the debate about any new technology, it is how you use it that's important. 和所有新技术的争论一样，真正重要的并不是技术本身，而是你如何使用它。
So let's look at some of its possible uses. 接下来让我们看看它的一些潜在用途。
Thanks to nanotechnology, there could be a major breakthrough in the field of transportation with the production of more durable metals. 借助纳米技术，交通运输领域可能会迎来重大突破，例如制造出更加耐用的金属材料。（breakthrough = 突破）
These could be virtually unbreakable, lighter and much more pliable leading to planes that are 50 times lighter than at present. 这些材料几乎坚不可摧，同时更轻、更有延展性，从而制造出重量仅为现有飞机五十分之一的飞机。（pliable = 柔韧的、可弯曲的）
Those same improved capabilities will dramatically reduce the cost of travelling into space making it more accessible to ordinary people and opening up a totally new holiday destination. 这些改进后的性能还将大幅降低太空旅行成本，使普通人也能负担得起，并开辟全新的旅游目的地。
In terms of technology, the computer industry will be able to shrink computer parts down to minute sizes. 在科技领域，计算机行业将能够把电脑零件缩小到极其微小的尺寸。（minute 这里读 /maɪˈnjuːt/，意为“极小的”）
We need nanotechnology in order to create a new generation of computers that will work even faster and will have a million times more memory but will be about the size of a sugar cube. 我们需要纳米技术来创造新一代计算机，它们运行速度更快，存储容量将达到目前的百万倍，但体积却只有一块方糖那么大。
Nanotechnology could also revolutionise the way that we generate power. 纳米技术还可能彻底改变我们发电的方式。（revolutionise = 彻底改变）
The cost of solar cells will be drastically reduced so harnessing this energy will be far more economical than at present. 太阳能电池的成本将大幅下降，因此利用太阳能将比现在经济得多。（harness energy = 利用能源）
But nanotechnology has much wider applications than this and could have an enormous impact on our environment. 不过，纳米技术的应用远不止这些，它还可能对环境产生巨大影响。
For instance, tiny airborne nano-robots could be programmed to actually rebuild the ozone layer, which could lessen the impact of global warming on our planet. 例如，微型空中纳米机器人可以被编程来修复臭氧层，从而减轻全球变暖对地球的影响。（IELTS易错：这里是设想，不是已实现技术）
That's a pretty amazing thought, isn't it? 这真是一个令人惊叹的设想，不是吗？
On a more local scale, this new technology could help with the clean-up of environmental disasters as nanotechnology will allow us to remove oil and other contaminants from the water far more effectively. 从更具体的层面来看，这项新技术还能帮助处理环境灾难，因为纳米技术能让我们更有效地清除水中的石油和其他污染物。（contaminants = 污染物）
And, if nanotechnology progresses as expected - as a sort of building block set of about 90 atoms - then you could build anything you wanted from the bottom up. 此外，如果纳米技术能够按预期发展，把大约90种原子当作积木一样使用，那么人们将能够从最基本的层面开始制造任何东西。（from the bottom up = 从最基础层面开始）
In terms of production, this means that you only use what you need and so there wouldn't be any waste. 从生产角度来看，这意味着只使用所需材料，因此不会产生任何浪费。
The notion that you could create anything at all has major implications for our health. “能够创造任何东西”这一概念，将对人类健康产生重大影响。（notion = 概念、想法）
It means that we'll eventually be able to replicate anything. 这意味着未来我们最终将能够复制任何东西。（replicate = 复制）
This would have a phenomenal effect on our society. 这将对整个社会产生极其巨大的影响。（phenomenal = 非凡的、惊人的）
In time it could even lead to the eradication of famine through the introduction of machines that produce food to feed the hungry. 随着时间推移，它甚至可能通过制造食物生产机器来消除饥荒。（eradication = 消灭、根除）
But it's in the area of medicine that nanotechnology may have its biggest impact. 不过，纳米技术影响最大的领域或许还是医学。
How we detect disease will change as tiny biosensors are developed to analyse tests in minutes rather than days. 随着微型生物传感器的发展，疾病检测方式将发生改变，因为检测结果可以在几分钟内完成分析，而不再需要几天时间。（biosensor = 生物传感器）
There's even speculation nano-robots could be used to slow the ageing process, lengthening life expectancy. 甚至有人推测，纳米机器人未来能够减缓衰老过程，从而延长人类寿命。（speculation = 推测；life expectancy = 预期寿命）
As you can see, I'm very excited by the implications that could be available to us in the next few decades. 正如你们所看到的，我对未来几十年纳米技术可能带来的影响感到十分兴奋。
Just how long it'll take, I honestly don't know. 至于这些设想究竟需要多久才能实现，说实话，我也不知道。
